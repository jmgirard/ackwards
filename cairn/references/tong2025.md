# tong2025 — Monte Carlo comparison placing bass-ackward as a factor-count method (backs the required-`k_max` rationale)

**Provenance.** Ingested 2026-07-23 by M70 from
`cairn/references/sources/tong2025.pdf` (gitignored). The shelf copy is the
**accepted preprint manuscript** (APA-style title page + running head
"COMPARISON FOR IDENTIFYING NUMBER OF FACTORS"), not the typeset journal
article — so the page anchors below are **preprint manuscript pages** and do
**not** match the published pagination (17–44).
Extraction: verified 2026-07-23 against the rendered preprint — the title (p. 1), abstract (p. 2), and introduction (p. 3) were read directly; no DOI is printed on the preprint, so the published DOI `10.1007/s40647-024-00423-2` was confirmed via Crossref — observed 2026-07-23.

**Citation.** Tong, L., Qu, W., & Zhang, Z. (2025). Comparison of the K1 rule,
parallel analysis, and the Bass-Ackward method on identifying the number of
factors in factor analysis. *Fudan Journal of the Humanities and Social
Sciences, 18*(1), 17–44. DOI `10.1007/s40647-024-00423-2` (online 2024-07-23;
Crossref-confirmed). The shelf copy is the preprint manuscript, not the typeset
article.

**Role.** Re-casts bass-ackward's **termination criterion** as a number-of-
factors rule and evaluates it by Monte Carlo simulation against the K1 rule and
parallel analysis. The paper is explicit that this is a narrow reading of a
method whose primary purpose is hierarchical structure (its own §"The
Bass-Ackward Method", preprint p. 8): the count falls out of *where the top-down
expansion stops* — "the number of factors depends on when to terminate the
top-down expansion" (preprint p. 9). It backs DESIGN §9's `k_max` = required
rationale ("force a deliberate choice; don't silently pick"): it is direct
simulation evidence that these stopping criteria are unreliable at recovering the
true count (BA-maxLoading 58%, PA 71%), which is why `ackwards` requires an
explicit `k_max` and retains **all** levels `1..k_max` rather than auto-selecting.
Also relevant to `suggest_k()` (D-013), which advises a range rather than one
number.

**`ackwards` implements neither criterion** — this is what the note guards
against a future reader assuming. BA-maxLoading (Goldberg's stop rule) is exactly
the automation the package declines (`k_max` required; every level retained for
inspection — `R/ackwards.R`). BA-cutoff's edge-thresholding *resembles* `prune()`'s
redundancy filter (`|r| ≥ redundancy_r`, optionally Tucker's φ), but `prune()`
runs **post-hoc on a fully-extracted hierarchy** to flag redundancy (the Forbes
2023 extension); it never terminates extraction or determines `k`.

## Extracted values

- The three methods compared — "the K1 rule and parallel analysis, along with a more recently developed method, the Bass-Ackward method", abstract, preprint p. 2.
- The two bass-ackward stopping criteria evaluated — "we examine two cessation criteria within the Bass-Ackward framework: BA-maxLoading and BA-cutoff", abstract, preprint p. 2. Defined preprint pp. 9–10: **BA-maxLoading** = Goldberg's rule (stop when a new factor is not the highest loading for any variable → retain k); **BA-cutoff** = stop when a new factor's inter-level correlations all fall below a set cutoff.
- Study framing — "a comprehensive comparative study of two widely used methods for determining the optimal number of factors in factor analysis ... along with a more recently developed method", abstract, preprint p. 2.
- Spelling — the paper writes "Bass-Ackward" (no medial *s*) throughout.
